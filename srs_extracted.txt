1. Introduction
1.1 Purpose
This Software Requirements Specification (SRS) defines the functional and nonfunctional requirements for the Hospital Management System Hospital Management System (HMS) and Hospital Alert System (HAS)  a cloud based, multitenant SaaS platform designed to digitize and centralize hospital operations in Bangladesh.
This document serves as the authoritative reference for stakeholder approval, product development, quality assurance, and system acceptance testing. It provides a complete description of what the system shall do, the constraints under which it shall operate, and the standards it must meet.

1.2 Intended Audience
This SRS is intended for the following audiences:
Project Sponsors & Investors for funding and go ahead approval
Product Owners & Business Analysts for scope validation
Software Architects & Development Team for technical design and implementation
QA Engineers for test plan creation and acceptance criteria
Hospital Administrators & Clinical Staff for requirement validation
Legal & Compliance Officers for regulatory and data privacy review

1.3 Intended Use
Hospital Management System HMS is intended to be used as a multitenant SaaS platform accessible via web browser and mobile devices as a Progressive Web App (PWA). Hospital administrators, doctors, nurses, lab technicians, pharmacists, receptionists, and patients will interact with the system through role specific dashboards. The platform is designed for day today operational use including patient registration, appointment scheduling, digital prescriptions, lab test management, pharmacy dispensing, emergency response, and billing.

1.4 Product Scope
Hospital Management System HMS is a 13 module integrated hospital management platform:
Hospital Alert System (HAS)  rule based real time multi channel alert engine (primary differentiator)
Patient Profile & Registration MRN generation, health history timeline, family account management
Appointment & Scheduling Management online booking, doctor availability, teleconsultation
Digital Prescription Management drug interaction checks, e signature, pharmacy forwarding
Lab Test Management  test booking, result entry, abnormal flagging, PDF report generation
Medicine & Pharmacy Management  prescription queue, inventory tracking, dispensing audit
Emergency & Ambulance Service SOS button, GPS tracking, ER pre notification
Bed & Ward Management  real time occupancy map, admission/discharge tracking
OPD & IPD Management patient status transitions, clinical notes, discharge summaries
Billing & Finance  automated itemized billing, multi gateway payments, revenue reporting
Doctor & Staff Management  RBAC, staff profiles, performance metrics
MultiTenant Architecture & SaaS Admin Panel  tenant lifecycle, subscription billing
Analytics & Reporting Dashboard  KPI dashboards, export functionality, pharma portal

The system targets hospitals, clinics, and diagnostic centers in Bangladesh, with primary coverage of Dhaka Metro and major cities .

Out of Scope (v1.0): AI/ML features, native iOS/Android mobile applications, IoT/smart device integration, multi country localization beyond Bangladesh, and legal e signature for patient consent forms.

2. Overall Description
2.1 User Needs
Hospital Management System HMS addresses identified pain points across five user segments:

2.1.1 Hospital Administrators
Centralized digital record of all patients, staff, appointments, and financial transactions
Real time ward and bed occupancy dashboards across all departments
Automated financial reporting for revenue, outstanding bills, and department performance
Single admin panel to manage multiple hospital branches under one account

2.1.2 Doctors & Clinical Staff
Digital prescription creation with automated drug interaction checking
Realtime patient health history and lab result access during consultations
Configurable alert rules for critical lab values and follow up reminders
Teleconsultation slot booking with automatic video link generation

2.1.3 Patients
Online appointment booking without requiring staff assistance
Digital access to prescriptions, lab reports, and complete health history timeline
Convenient bill payment via bKash, Nagad, or bank card
Automated reminders for appointments, follow ups, and medication refills

2.1.4 Lab Technicians & Pharmacists
Streamlined test result entry with automated critical value flagging and HAS alert triggering
Digital prescription queue from doctor’s module with inventory auto deduction on dispensing
Low stock alerts and expiry date tracking for pharmacy inventory management

2.1.5 Emergency & Security Personnel
One click SOS emergency activation accessible from any portal screen
Live ambulance GPS tracking and real time dispatch management from the emergency dashboard
Automated ER pre notification with patient MRN, vitals, and estimated arrival time




2.2 Assumptions and Dependencies
2.2.1 Assumptions
All target hospitals have active internet connectivity (broadband or 4G mobile data)
Hospital administrators own a smartphone and have basic digital literacy
SSL Wireless, Twilio, bKash, Nagad, and SendGrid APIs will remain commercially available throughout v1.0 lifecycle
SMS delivery rates in Bangladesh will remain sufficient for OTP, alert, and notification use cases
Users will primarily access Hospital Management System via browser or PWA; native app is a v2.0 consideration
Hospital administrators will complete system configuration before clinical staff onboarding
Multi language Bangla content management beyond UI labels is a v2.0 consideration

2.2.2 Dependencies
AWS (EKS, RDS PostgreSQL, S3, CloudFront)  primary cloud infrastructure provider
bKash Merchant API  mobile payment gateway (requires bKash merchant account)
Nagad Developer API  secondary mobile payment gateway

3. System Features and Requirements
3.1 Functional Requirements
Requirements are stated using EARS format: “When [event], the system shall [response]” and validated with Gherkin BDD scenarios.

3.1.1 Module 1  Hospital Alert System (HAS)
HAS is the primary differentiator of the Hospital Management System platform. It is a rulebased engine that monitors system events and dispatches alerts through multiple channels.

FR HAS 01 When a lab technician saves a critical lab result, the system shall dispatch a multi channel alert to the ordering doctor and patient within 60 seconds.

FR HAS 02  When a Hospital Admin configures an alert threshold rule, the system shall apply the rule to all new results immediately without developer intervention.

FR HAS 03  When a ward’s bed occupancy reaches the configured threshold (default: 90%), the system shall trigger an alert to the Hospital Director and Admin via email and SMS within 60 seconds.

FR HAS 04  When an appointment is confirmed, the system shall send automated reminder alerts to the patient 24 hours and 2 hours before the scheduled time.

FR HAS 05  When a doctor sets a patient follow up reminder, the system shall automatically alert the patient at the configured date via SMS.

FR HAS 06  When a primary SMS gateway (SSL Wireless) fails, the system shall automatically retry using the secondary gateway (Twilio) within 30 seconds.

Scenario: SMS gateway failover
   Given SSL Wireless returns a failure response for an alert dispatch
   When the failure is detected
   Then the system shall retry delivery via Twilio within 30 seconds and log the failover attempt

3.1.2 Module 2  Patient Profile & Registration
FR PAT 01  When a new patient registers, the system shall generate a unique Medical Record Number (MRN) in format HAX XXXXX and send an SMS confirmation within 30 seconds.

Scenario: Patient self registration
   Given a new patient submits the registration form with valid details
   When OTP verification is completed
   Then the system shall generate a unique MRN, create the patient profile, and send an SMS confirmation within 30 seconds

FR PAT 02  When a patient profile is created, the system shall store: full name, date of birth, NID number, gender, blood group, phone, email, emergency contacts, and profile photo, with NID uniqueness enforced.

FR PAT 03  When a receptionist registers a walk in patient, the system shall allow on behalf registration and send the patient an SMS confirmation.

FR PAT 04  When a guardian account is linked to patient profiles, the system shall allow one guardian to manage up to 10 linked profiles, each with an independent MRN.

FR PAT 05  When a patient views their health history timeline, the system shall display all visits, diagnoses, prescriptions, lab results, and reports in reverse chronological order within 2 seconds.

3.1.3 Module 3  Appointment & Scheduling Management
FR APT 01  When a patient books an appointment online, the system shall display real time doctor availability, prevent double booking, and send an SMS confirmation within 60 seconds.

Scenario: Online appointment booking
   Given a patient selects a doctor, date, and available time slot
   When the booking is confirmed
   Then the slot shall be locked immediately, the appointment saved, and an SMS confirmation sent to the patient within 60 seconds

FR APT 02  When a doctor defines their schedule, the system shall allow configuration of available hours, break times, and appointment duration per specialty, displaying only configured slots to patients.

FR APT 03  When a receptionist cancels or reschedules an appointment, the system shall record the action, notify the patient via SMS, and release the slot immediately.

FR APT 04  When a teleconsultation slot is booked, the system shall automatically generate a browser video link and deliver it to both patient and doctor via SMS and email.

3.1.4 Module 4  Digital Prescription Management
FR PRE 01  When a doctor generates a digital prescription, the system shall capture diagnosis, drug name, dosage, frequency, duration, and special instructions, apply the doctor’s e signature, and forward it to the pharmacy module within 5 seconds.

Scenario: Prescription creation and pharmacy forwarding
   Given a doctor completes and saves a prescription
   When the prescription is signed
   Then the prescription shall appear in the pharmacy queue within 5 seconds and the patient shall receive a PDF copy via SMS link within 60 seconds

FR PRE 02  When a new medicine is added to a prescription, the system shall automatically check for drug drug interactions and display a warning within 2 seconds; the doctor must acknowledge before proceeding.

FR PRE 03  When a prescription is saved, the system shall store a hash of the prescription content; any post save modification shall invalidate the digital signature.

FR PRE 04  When a doctor searches the drug database, the system shall return results including generic name, brand name, dosage, and contraindications within 1 second from a database of at least 5,000 entries.

3.1.5 Module 5  Lab Test Management
FR LAB 01  When a lab technician saves a test result, the system shall automatically flag abnormal values (Red for Critical, Amber for Borderline) and trigger an HAS alert to the ordering doctor and patient within 60 seconds.

Scenario: Lab result with critical value
   Given a lab technician enters a result value exceeding the Critical threshold
   When the result is saved
   Then the result shall be flagged Red, an HAS alert dispatched to the doctor and patient within 60 seconds, and the report added to the patient’s health history timeline within 30 seconds

FR LAB 02  When a lab report is generated, the system shall produce a professional PDF within 10 seconds including hospital branding, technician name, reference ranges, and abnormal value flags.

FR LAB 03  When a Lab Admin configures reference ranges, the system shall allow configuration per test type, age group, and gender, applying flags dynamically based on patient demographics.

3.1.6 Module 6  Medicine & Pharmacy Management
FR PHM 01  When a doctor saves a prescription, the system shall automatically add it to the pharmacy dispensing queue within 5 seconds, displaying patient name, MRN, and prescribed drugs.

FR PHM 02  When medicine is dispensed to a patient, the system shall automatically deduct the dispensed quantity from inventory within 2 seconds and create a timestamped audit log entry.

Scenario: Medicine dispensing and inventory deduction
   Given a pharmacist dispenses 2 units of a drug from a prescription
   When the dispense action is confirmed
   Then inventory shall decrease by 2 units, a log entry created with pharmacist ID, timestamp, and patient MRN

FR PHM 03  When any drug’s stock falls below the configured minimum threshold, the system shall display a low stock alert on the pharmacy dashboard.

FR PHM 04  When a drug is out of stock, the pharmacist shall be able to log partial dispensing with a note; the patient shall be notified and the outstanding quantity tracked.

3.1.7 Module 7  Emergency & Ambulance Service
FR EMR 01  When a patient activates the SOS emergency button (accessible within 2 clicks from any screen), the system shall alert the hospital emergency dispatcher with patient name, MRN, phone, and last known location within 30 seconds.

Scenario: SOS emergency activation
   Given a patient presses the SOS button on the patient portal
   When the SOS flow activates
   Then the dispatcher shall receive an alert within 30 seconds and the emergency dashboard shall show the new request

FR EMR 02  When a dispatcher assigns an ambulance, the system shall enable real time GPS tracking of the dispatched ambulance visible to the patient/family and the ER team, with location updates every 10 seconds.

FR EMR 03  When an ambulance is dispatched, the system shall send the ER team a pre notification alert including patient MRN, last known vitals, known allergies, and estimated arrival time before the ambulance arrives.

3.1.8 Module 8  Bed & Ward Management
FR BED 01  When a bed assignment or release occurs, the system shall update the real time digital ward map showing all bed statuses (Available / Occupied / Reserved / Under Maintenance) within 5 seconds.

Scenario: Bed assignment on patient admission
   Given a receptionist assigns Bed B 204 to an admitted patient
   When the assignment is saved
   Then bed B 204 status shall change to ‘Occupied’ on the ward map within 5 seconds, recording admission date, admitting doctor, and expected discharge date

FR BED 02  When any ward reaches the configured capacity threshold (default: 90%), the system shall trigger an HAS alert to the Hospital Director within 60 seconds.

3.1.9 Module 9  OPD & IPD Management
FR OPD 01  When a patient’s status transitions (Registration → OPD → IPD Admitted → Discharged), the system shall record the change with a timestamp and the actor’s user ID.

FR OPD 02  When an IPD patient’s nurse records vitals (temperature, blood pressure, pulse, oxygen saturation), the system shall store the entry with a timestamp and display a graphical trend on the patient care chart.

FR OPD 03  When a patient is discharged, the system shall auto generate a discharge summary PDF within 10 seconds containing diagnosis, treatment, prescriptions, and follow up instructions, and add it to the health history timeline.

3.1.10 Module 10  Billing & Finance
FR BIL 01  When a billing session is opened for a patient, the system shall automatically aggregate all charges (doctor fee, lab fees, medicine cost, bed rental, procedure fees) into a single itemized bill within 3 seconds.

Scenario: Automated bill aggregation
   Given a patient with completed OPD consultation, 2 lab tests, and 3 dispensed medicines
   When the receptionist opens the billing screen
   Then all line items shall be auto populated within 3 seconds with accurate fees for each service

FR BIL 02  When a patient completes payment via bKash, Nagad, Stripe, or cash, the system shall record the transaction, update bill status, and generate a PDF receipt within 5 seconds.

FR BIL 03  When a partial payment is recorded, the system shall log the amount paid and display the remaining outstanding balance on the patient billing screen.

FR BIL 04  When an authorized Hospital Admin applies a discount to a bill, the system shall record the discount with an audit log entry; unauthorized roles shall not be able to apply discounts.

3.1.11 Module 11  Doctor & Staff Management
FR STF 01  When a Hospital Admin creates a staff account, the system shall enforce the configured RBAC role, restricting the user to only the modules and data permitted for their role.

Scenario: Tenant cannot access admin billing management
   Given a logged in Nurse user
   When they attempt to navigate to the Billing Management module
   Then the system shall display an ‘Access Denied’ error and log the attempt

FR STF 02  When a staff member fails authentication 5 consecutive times, the system shall lock the account for 15 minutes and notify the registered email.

FR STF 03  When a Hospital Admin views the doctor performance dashboard, the system shall display total patients seen, total revenue generated, and average appointment duration, exportable as CSV.

3.1.12 Module 12  Multi Tenant Architecture & SaaS Admin Panel
FR TNT 01  When a tenant is provisioned, the system shall enforce complete data isolation at the database row level security (RLS) layer, preventing any cross tenant data access.

Scenario: Cross tenant data isolation
   Given Hospital A and Hospital B are separate tenants
   When a Hospital A admin queries patient records
   Then the system shall return only Hospital A’s records; Hospital B data shall never be accessible

FR TNT 02  When a tenant is created, the system shall provision a unique subdomain (format: hospitalname.Hospital Management System.com.bd) with configurable branding including logo and hospital name.

FR TNT 03  When a tenant’s payment is 7 days overdue, the system shall automatically suspend their access while retaining data for 30 days before deletion, offering a data export during the grace period.

FR TNT 04  When an invoice remains unpaid 7 days past its due date, the system shall send a payment reminder notification to the Hospital Admin and flag the subscription as overdue in the SaaS Admin Panel.

3.1.13 Module 13  Analytics & Reporting Dashboard
FR ANL 01  When a Hospital Admin views the analytics dashboard, the system shall display total revenue, total patients, top doctors by patient count, and most ordered lab tests, loadable within 3 seconds and filterable by date range.

FR ANL 02  When an Admin requests a report export, the system shall generate the file in CSV or PDF format within 10 seconds for reports up to 10,000 rows.

FR ANL 03  When a pharmaceutical company accesses the pharma portal, the system shall display only anonymized, aggregated prescription data (drug totals by doctor); patient name, NID, and MRN shall never be visible.

3.2.3 Usability Requirements
The system shall achieve a System Usability Scale (SUS) score of 70 or above in user testing with non technical users (receptionists, nurses, patients).
A new receptionist must be able to complete patient registration in under 5 minutes after reading the documentation, without additional training.
All core workflows (register patient, book appointment, dispense medicine, pay bill) shall be completable in under 3 minutes by a first time user.
The platform shall be fully responsive and functional on screen widths from 320px (mobile) to 2560px (large desktop).
All interactive elements must be keyboard navigable and meet WCAG 2.1 Level AA accessibility standards.
Error messages shall be written in plain language (not technical codes) with a suggested corrective action.

3.2.4 Reliability Requirements
The system shall maintain 99.9% uptime SLA (excluding planned maintenance windows), measured monthly, equivalent to < 44 minutes unplanned downtime per month.
HAS alert delivery success rate shall be at or above 99.5% of all dispatched alerts.
Recovery Time Objective (RTO): system restoration after failure within 1 hour.
Recovery Point Objective (RPO): maximum data loss window of 4 hours.
In the event of a payment gateway outage, the system shall display a user friendly error and queue the failed transaction for retry, without double charging the user.
The system shall perform automated database backups every 4 hours, retained with point in time recovery for 7 days.

3.2.5 Compliance Requirements
The system shall comply with Bangladesh Digital Security Act (DSA) 2018 requirements for data handling, user consent, and breach notification to CIRT Bangladesh within 72 hours of discovery.
All financial transaction records shall be retained for a minimum of 7 years in accordance with Bangladesh National Board of Revenue (NBR) requirements.
bKash and Nagad API integrations shall comply with Bangladesh Bank Mobile Financial Services (MFS) regulations and merchant onboarding requirements.
BMDC (Bangladesh Medical and Dental Council) doctor registration numbers shall be validated at staff onboarding.
The system architecture shall be designed to be HIPAA ready with appropriate access controls, encryption, and audit trails.
Patient health data shall be stored on servers within Bangladesh or in a jurisdiction with equivalent data protection standards.

4. Other Requirements

4.1 Database Requirements
Primary database: PostgreSQL 16 hosted on AWS RDS with Multi AZ deployment and schema level multi tenancy.
Row Level Security (RLS) policies enforced at the PostgreSQL layer for complete tenant data isolation; penetration tested before launch.
Database schema shall enforce foreign key constraints, indexed lookups on patient_id, tenant_id, appointment_id, lab_id, and invoice_id fields.
Automated database backups every 4 hours, stored in AWS S3 with versioning enabled; point in time recovery enabled for up to 7 days of rollback.
Database connections shall use connection pooling (maximum 100 connections per instance).
All patient health records and financial transaction records shall be stored in append only tables with no update or delete permissions at application level.

4.2 Legal and Regulatory Requirements
Bangladesh Digital Security Act (DSA) 2018: User data shall not be sold or shared with third parties without explicit consent. Data breach notification to CIRT Bangladesh within 72 hours of discovery.
National Board of Revenue (NBR) Compliance: All financial transaction records and receipts shall be retained for a minimum of 7 years and be exportable for tax audit purposes.
Bangladesh Bank Payment Guidelines: bKash and Nagad integrations shall comply with Bangladesh Bank’s Mobile Financial Services (MFS) regulations and merchant onboarding requirements.
BMDC Compliance: Doctor BMDC registration numbers shall be validated at staff account creation; prescriptions shall carry the BMDC number of the prescribing doctor.
HIPAA Readiness: Although Bangladesh does not mandate HIPAA, Hospital Management System shall implement HIPAA aligned access controls, encryption, and audit trails as best practice for future regional expansion.
GDPR Adjacent Provisions: Hospital Management System shall implement right to erasure requests, data export capability, and clear privacy policy as best practice for future regional expansion.

5.2.2 System Architecture Overview
Hospital Management System HMS follows a cloud native, containerized three tier architecture:
Presentation Layer: Next.js 14 PWA hosted on AWS CloudFront CDN  serves responsive web dashboards for all eight user roles; installable as PWA on any device
Application Layer: Laravel 11 REST API running on AWS EKS (Kubernetes with Docker containers)  handles all business logic, RBAC, HAS alert engine, and third party integrations; auto scales at 70% CPU utilization
Data Layer: AWS RDS PostgreSQL 16 (Multi AZ) for relational data with Row Level Security; AWS S3 / Wasabi for PDF reports, prescription documents, and patient photos; Redis for session management and HAS alert job queuing

External integrations communicate outbound from the Application Layer via HTTPS to bKash API, Nagad API, Stripe, SendGrid, SSL Wireless, Twilio, and Pusher. All inbound traffic is routed through AWS Application Load Balancer with WAF enabled. WebSocket connections (Pusher/Laravel Echo) provide real time updates for alert delivery, bed status, and GPS tracking.
